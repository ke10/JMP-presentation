Motivation

Agenda-setter makes a policy proposal that must be approved by voters.

The proposal can be revised when it gets rejected.

  • The president nominates federal judges, approved by senators
  • Chairs make motions that, approved by committee members
  • Municipal authorities propose spending measures, approved by eligible voters

Key features

Voters are privately informed about their ideal policies.

Voters have conflicting preferences.

Questions

Does the agenda-setter value the ability to revise proposals?

How the adopted policies depend on the preferences of voters?

What is the role of voting rule?

Environment

  • Two voters, $L$ and $R$, and the agenda-setter $A$
  • The policy space is $\mathbb R_+$, the status-quo policy is $0$
  • The agenda-setter's period utility from policy $x$ is $u_A(x)=x$
  • The utilities of voters depend on state $\omega\in\{\ell,h\}$

Quadratic utilities

Period utility of voter $i$ from policy $x$ in state $\omega$ is \[u_i(x;\omega)=-\textstyle\left(\frac12 y_i^{\omega}-x\right)^2\]

Assumptions

Denote the ideal policies of voter $L$ using $y^\ell>0$ and $y^h>0$.

Assume that the ideal policies of voter $R$ are $y^\ell+b$ and $y^h+b$ for $b\geq0$.

Assume $y^\ell < y^h$.
